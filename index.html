On December 4, 2024, a shocking event captured the nation's attention: the CEO of UnitedHealthcare, Brian Thompson, was murdered in Manhattan.

Yet, the public's reaction was unsettlingly numb, with many cracking jokes about his death.

Instead, the spotlight shone on the suspected murderer, Luigi Mangione, who was elevated to a hero status across the internet, celebrated through countless memes and viral content.

How does the public's reaction to this murder reflect deeper issues within the American healthcare system, particularly in the insurance sector dominated by giants like UnitedHealthcare?

This phenomenon of adoration towards a figure associated with such a violent act may seem perplexing at first glance. However, we seek to inspect various facets related to the complex relationship between the public's sentiment and the issues within the American healthcare system, particularly focusing on the industry-dominating UnitedHealthcare.

Exploring Denied Claims: Which company reaps the most benefits in 2023?

Following the scrutiny of healthcare quality, another critical issue emerges: the alarming rate of denied claims by insurance companies. Let's delve into the denial patterns among leading insurers, exploring how such practices boost their profits at the expense of consumers. We aim to expose the struggles of policyholders who frequently have to fight for the coverage they paid for and deserve. This ongoing battle not only exacerbates financial burdens but also deepens the public's mistrust and resentment towards these powerful healthcare entities.

Kaiser Permanente

Reported Revenue in 2023: $100.8B

Percent of denied claims: 7%

One case shows a policyholder of Kaiser Permanente being denied therapy treatment for PTSD caused by a car accident. She shared, "They denied my claim. I am out $300 a month for therapy when I pay for health insurance that should cover it." (National Union of Healthcare Workers)

Aetna

Reported Revenue in 2023: $186.8B

Percent of denied claims: 20%

Aetna is also guilty of denying claims for life-saving procedures. In one case, a policyholder shared "she was denied a CT Scan for months by Aetna. She shared that her doctor fought the good fight and appealed again and again. Finally she got the scan she needed. But, by then, the cancer had spread. (WV Citizen Action Group)

Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield

Reported Revenue in 2023: $171.3B

Percent of denied claims: 23%

In another case of a denied claim, an Anthem policyholder shared they suffered from cardiac arrest, for which she was transported to the closest possible hospital by air ambulance. She said, "If it weren't for the helicopter, I'd be dead. They were denying the claim because it didn't get preapproved." (Khou News)

UnitedHealthcare

Reported Revenue in 2023: $371.6B Average

Percent of denied claims: 32%

UnitedHealthcare displays the highest financial benefit from the highest rate of denied claims among competitors.

Taking a closer look, we see one patient share "[UnitedHealthcare] denied my surgery two days before it was scheduled. I was in the hospital finance office in tears (when I was supposed to be at the hospital doing pre-op stuff). My mother was flying out to see me. My surgeon spent a day and a half pleading my case to United when she probably should have been taking care of her other patients." (CNN)

By inspecting these pain points, it becomes more clear how many issues Americans face in their health care, especially when compared with other dveloped countries. Among the various companies, UnitedHealthcare shows a pattern of high profit but poor service, from having the highest revenue among top insurance companies, to displaying the highest rates of denied claims. These growing issues shed light on the general public's outcry against UnitedHealthcare.
